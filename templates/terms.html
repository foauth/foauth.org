Terms of Service

We hate complicated terms of service too, so let's keep this simple, shall we? If anything seems out of place, you don't have to sign up, but please tell us what you don't like, because we want to know if you think we're doing something sleazy.

What foauth.org will do

  • Store OAuth tokens for you so you don't have to deal with them.
  • Store your foauth.org user information, including your password.
  • Link those two items so you can log in to your services.
  • Provide an endpoint for you to use for each service.
  • Sign all your API traffic according to the OAuth standards.
  • Dutifully pass all other API traffic through to your services.
  • Be as reliable as its maintainers can manage.

What foauth.org won't do

  • See or store your password from your favorite services.
  • Share your foauth.org user information with anyone.
  • Share your OAuth tokens with anyone.
  • Monitor or store any of your API traffic, unless you opt-in to features that do so.
  • Monitor or throttle the number of API calls you use.
  • Guarantee any specific amount of uptime or reliability.
  • Require you to sign up for anything other than foauth.org.

What you will do

  • Use HTTP Basic authentication to access your favorite services.
  • Stay within the API usage limits of those services.
  • Respond promptly to any concerns we raise about your usage.

What you won't do

  • Share your information with anyone.
  • Violate the policies or terms of use for our suppported services.
  • Blame us if using our service harms you or your data in any way.

What we hope you'll do

These aren't part of the official terms of service, so you don't have to agree to do anything on this final list. But we're working hard to bring you a service you'll enjoy, and it'd be awesome if you like it so much that you'd want to do something more.

  • Tell your friends how much you love foauth.org and encourage them to sign up as well.
  • Share what you've built on foauth.org, so we and your fellow users can learn from what you've done.
  • Suggest new services for use with foauth.org. We can't possibly find them all, so feel free to submit your own.
  • Contribute to foauth.org itself. The source code is freely available, and even if you can't help with the code, we're open to suggestions.
